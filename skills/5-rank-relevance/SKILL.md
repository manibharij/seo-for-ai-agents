# Rank — good and relevant enough to win

**Rung 5 of the Visibility Ladder, and the whole point of it.** Reach, Read, Understand and Connect make a page *eligible* to rank. Rank is whether it actually *deserves* to, for the searches its audience makes. This is the SEO summit. Above it sits only the optional AEO layer (`cite-aeo-geo`), which is for AI-answer citation, after a page already ranks.

> **Honest boundary, stated up front.** Final ranking is decided partly by things you do not control: off-site authority (links, brand, reputation) and the strength of the competition. Those are not build-time, owned-media work (see the Report step and `METHOD.md`'s boundary). This rung does everything on *your own site* that earns ranking, taken as far as it goes. That is the part you control, and it's substantial.

**Self-discovery, not interrogation.** Work out the page's target intent yourself, from its content, purpose, headings, and place in the site. Don't ask the user "what keyword is this for"; infer it, and say what you inferred.

Work the four steps: **Diagnose → Fix → Verify → Report.** The deep work is often done by the content/strategy skills; this rung is where it's assessed and prioritised against the goal of ranking.

---

## Step 1 — Diagnose

For each important page, on the served content:
- **Search intent** — infer the dominant intent (informational / commercial / transactional / navigational) the page should serve, then check the content actually serves it. Mismatch (a thin product page for an informational query, or an essay where a buyer wants a price) is the most common ranking failure.
- **Quality and depth** — is the page comprehensive, specific, and genuinely more useful than what currently ranks, or thin, generic, derivative? (Lean on `seo-content-audit` for the corpus view.)
- **E-E-A-T and trust** — is there real experience, expertise, named authorship, and cited sources, the signals engines weigh before ranking a page (and weigh heavily for YMYL topics)? Anonymous, unsupported content is a weak candidate.
- **Topical authority** — does the site cover this topic deeply enough (the hub-and-spoke clusters from Connect) to be a credible source, or is this a lone page on an island?
- **Relevance signals** — do the title, `<h1>`, headings and body clearly align with the intent, in the audience's language (without keyword-stuffing)?
- **Live data, if connected (optional, never required):** Search Console shows pages that get impressions but few clicks (an intent/title gap) and queries you nearly rank for (quick opportunities); a keyword tool shows real demand and how strong the competition is. Use them to target real opportunities; without them, infer from the content and the domain and say so. See `references/ranking-factors.md`.

## Step 2 — Fix

These are owned-media improvements; dispatch the deep work to the content/strategy skills and keep it strictly white-hat.
- **Align to intent** — restructure the page so it answers the query it should, leading with the answer. (Via `seo-content-editing`, which edits real content and never fabricates.)
- **Strengthen quality and depth** — improve and deepen from real material; where substance is genuinely missing, brief the human, don't invent it. (Via `seo-content-audit` actions: improve / consolidate / refresh, or a create-brief for a gap.)
- **Surface real E-E-A-T** — bring genuine authorship, credentials and sources to the surface and mirror them in schema (Understand). Where they don't exist, flag as a human task; never fabricate an author, credential, or citation.
- **Build topical authority** — plan and wire the hub-and-spoke clusters (`seo-positioning-strategy` + Connect) so the site is credibly deep on the topic.
- **Tune relevance** — make the title, headings and content match the intent in real language; remove off-intent padding.

## Step 3 — Verify (on the served output)

- The page clearly **targets and satisfies its intent** in the served HTML (read the opening in isolation: does it answer the query?).
- Quality and depth are genuinely there, not padded; nothing fabricated.
- **E-E-A-T signals are real and present** in the served output and in schema.
- The relevant cluster exists and links coherently (topical authority is real, not asserted).
- **Honesty check:** nothing was invented to look more authoritative or comprehensive than the site truly is.

## Step 4 — Report (plain English) + the boundary

1. **What was holding it back** — e.g. "this page is reachable and readable, but it answers a different question than the one people search, and it has no named author on a topic where that matters."
2. **What was improved** — intent alignment, depth, surfaced E-E-A-T, topical authority, each tied to earning ranking, with proof on the served output.
3. **What needs the user** — genuine substance, authors, or sources you refused to fabricate.
4. **The boundary, plainly:** you've now done the owned-media work that earns ranking. **Whether the page actually ranks also depends on off-site authority and the competition, which are not build-time work** (earning links and reputation is a separate, earned-media discipline; tracking where you actually rank is live data). This rung takes the part you control as far as it goes.

This is the top of the ladder. Once a page genuinely ranks, the optional **`cite-aeo-geo`** layer makes the same page eligible to be cited by AI answers, on top, never instead.
